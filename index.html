Methodology

Data Sources & Approach

  • Addresses: 278 unique Ohio addresses from the FBCJ ServantKeeper directory (3 geographic outliers >35 mi excluded, 10 out-of-state excluded, 1 geocode failure)
  • Geocoding: US Census Bureau geocoder (primary), Nominatim ZIP centroid fallback (~14 addresses)
  • Driving data: Google Maps Distance Matrix API with Sunday 9:00 AM EDT departure time for church-relevant traffic patterns
  • Contour maps: 40×40 grid (1,600 points) centered symmetrically on the midpoint between the two churches, covering a 20-mile radius. Smoothed with median filter + Gaussian blur to remove routing noise
  • Probability model: Logistic function: P(Jackson) = 1 / (1 + exp(K × (time_to_Jackson − time_to_Dalton))), where K=0.15 per minute. This is an assumed behavioral model, not fitted to actual attendance outcomes
  • Statistical tests: Wilcoxon signed-rank (is median time diff ≠ 0?) and binomial test (is the split ≠ 50/50?), reported for both all data and high-quality only
  • Sensitivity analysis: K tested at 0.05–0.30; incumbency bias tested at 0–15 minutes

The K Parameter

K controls how sharply people respond to driving time differences. At K=0.15, a 10-minute time advantage gives ~82% probability for the closer church. K is assumed, not measured from data. The sensitivity analysis shows which conclusions hold across all reasonable K values and which are parameter-dependent.

Incumbency Bias

The base model treats Jackson and Dalton as interchangeable — equal driving time = 50/50. In reality, current members have relationships, habits, and ministry involvement at Jackson. Incumbency bias adds a switching cost: "Dalton must be X minutes closer before it's a fair fight." The sensitivity analysis shows results at 0, 5, 10, and 15 minute bias levels. Church planting research suggests 15–20 minutes of advantage is typically needed to overcome inertia.

Data Quality

  • 260 high-quality addresses: exact geocode + Google Maps routing
  • 14 low-quality addresses: ZIP centroid location and/or straight-line distance estimate (1.3× road factor, ~30 mph assumed)
  • Low-quality entries are marked with gray dashed circles on maps and italicized in the Excel export
  • Statistical tests are reported both ways (all data vs. high-quality only) to ensure low-quality points don't drive conclusions

Important Notes

  • This analysis answers "which current members might prefer Dalton?" — not "is Dalton a good location for a church plant?" (the latter requires population, demographic, and competing-church data)
  • The contour maps show objective geographic data (which church is closer from any point) — these are the most reliable visualizations
  • Driving times use EDT departure; during EST months (Nov–Mar) actual times may differ by ±1–2 minutes
  • The model uses driving time only — it does not account for social ties, pastoral relationships, ministry involvement, or worship style preferences
